You are being asked to participate in a research study. The purpose of the study is to examine how individuals acting as supply chain managers view the forecast results provided by a statistical forecast system. Taking part in this study is voluntary, and choosing not to take part will not affect your standing in any employment, class or other. The investigators will explain the study to you and will answer any questions you might have. It is your choice whether you take part in this study. If you agree to participate and then choose to withdraw from the study, that is your right, and your decision will not be held against you.

Your participation in this research study involves indicating whether you accept the predictions provided by a decision support system and surveys. The questions will be presented in an online experiment. This is a one-time activity and there will be no direct interaction with the investigators or with other participants. The experiment involves questions such as “Week 34 forecast value: 377 units. Will you keep it?” and will take you approximately 20 minutes.

This research study is not expected to pose any additional risks beyond what you would normally experience in your regular everyday life. However, the possible risks specific to this research study include those similar to a person’s everyday use of the Internet. Study data will be physically and electronically secured by the research team. As with any use of electronic means to store data, there is a risk of breach of data security. The research team will store your personal information in a separate and secure location away from your study data and maintain your confidentiality in all publications/presentations to do with this research. Access to research study records will be limited to people who have a need to review this information. All paper and electronic data collected from this study will be stored in a secure location on the UNT campus and/or a secure UNT server for at least three (3) years past the end of this research in a password protected computer in PI’s campus office.

UNT does not provide medical services, or financial assistance for emotional distress or injuries that might happen from participating in this research. If you do experience any discomfort, please inform the research team. If your need is urgent, helpful resources include the National Suicide and Crisis hotline at 988.

Every participant will receive a base compensation of $3 contingent upon passing all attention checks, plus a performance-based bonus of up to $2. The top 33.33% will receive a bonus payment of $2; the middle 33.33% will receive a bonus payment of $1; the bottom 33.33% will receive no bonus payment. As a result, your total compensation will range from $3 to $5. Your bonus will be determined by your performance during the forecast decision periods, as measured by your Cumulative Mean Absolute Percentage Error (CMAPE). Performance-based bonuses will be calculated and paid after all participants have completed all tasks in the study. At the end of the study, you must report the completion code on Prolific. Failure to submit the completion code will result in non-payment for your participation.

Compensation for participation is not being provided by the University of North Texas researchers. Upon experiment completion, you will be compensated in the amount previously agreed upon when you started the experiment. Any compensation is directly provided by the platform you participate from. If you choose not to complete part or all of study procedures, or if you fail attention checks, you may not be compensated for participation. If you have questions or would like to discuss issues regarding your compensation, please contact the survey platform. You may also discuss this with the research team at paul.owusu@unt.edu.

If you have any questions about the study, you may contact the investigators listed above. Any questions you have regarding your rights as a research subject, or complaints about the research may be directed to the Office of Research Integrity and Compliance at 940-565-4643, or by email at untirb@unt.edu.
